Loose Capsule Cohesion (LCC)

This metric is defined as the relative number of directly or indirectly connected operations. An operation O1 is directly connected to another operation O2, if both O1 and O2 or their corresponding call trees access at least one common variable. Two operations are indirectly connected if they are not directly connected to each other, but they are directly connected to a common third operation. LCC counts the unique pairs of operations that are directly or indirectly connected and divides this number to the number of possible unique pairs of operations in the capsule, which is n*(n-1)/2, where n is the number of operations in the capsule.

  Known thresholds
Capsules with LCC<0.5 are considered non-cohesive. A capsule with LCC=0.8 is considered "quite cohesive", while a capsule with LCC=1 is maximally cohesive.
